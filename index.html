What is Miles for Matias?

Miles for Matias is an annual walk hosted by the Theta Kappa Chapter of Alpha Phi Omega at Binghamton University in honor of a late brother, Tom Matias. Tom cared greatly about the research that The Lourie Center for Pediatric MS and Stony Brook Children's Hospital did. In his honor, for the past 8 years we have done a walk to raise money and awareness for the disease.

About Pediatric Multiple Sclerosis

Multiple Sclerosis is a autoimmune neurodegenerative disease where the initial symptoms may be mild and non-specific. The symptoms that are similar between children and adults can include weakness, tingling, muscle spasms, tremors, and problems with walking. Children also might have seizures, but because the symptoms are non-specifc, the disease can go undiagnosised until the disease progresses.

About The Lourie Center for Pediatric MS

The Lourie Center for Pediatric MS was established to advance the recognition, evaluation and treatment of pediatric multiple sclerosis (MS) through the creation of a multidisciplinary program dedicated to clinical care and scientific research of children and adolescents with MS. All the proceeds of the walk go directly to the Lourie Center to help fund their research and treatment at their facility at Stony Brook's Childrens Hospital.
